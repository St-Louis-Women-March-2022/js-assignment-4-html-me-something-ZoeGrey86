About Zoe Grey

"I love to make things, I guess that's part of being a Smith. And from my love of making things springs my love of art. Ever since I was little I've found joy in drawing, painting, or occasionally sculpting. Though I never had any ambition to make my living from my art, I do enjoy sharing my art with anyone willing to look. So if you care to subject yourself to a selection of my artwork from way back to up until present, please continue and enjoy. "

Marilyn Monroe

A pencil drawing of Norma Jean Baker, better know as Marylin Monroe. the photo this was drwn from was taken in 1956 though I didn't draw this until 2006, just sixty years later. Marylin was such an icon and I've always admired her, though I wouldn't fully understand why until some years later.

HeartBreaker, 2007

HighSchool was a time of much heart ache. As you can see in this colored pencil and ink drawing with it's bright colors, dark lines and vivid imagery a sort of toonish style that I develop a love/hate relationship with, that I often return to. Though HighSchool is long over I still enjoy looking at this picture from time to time as a way of reminding myself that I once cared about stupid little things, and all things come to pass.

Screamer, 2009

The years after HighSchool didn't get to much better. A lack of direction, a tragic falling out with friends, and supressing who I am and wanted to be left me pretty severally depressed. You can see in this pencil sketch on paper some of the anguish I internalized for decades. In denial and afraid of myself, it can still bring tears to my eyes to remember the level of self loathing I subjected myself to.

UnNamed, 2019

This series was a fun night, one who's influnces are a story for another time. But in this mixed media triplet you can get a feel of the wild abandon and free spirit that was flowing that night. The elegance of the women in gowns providing a stark contrast to the abstract background that nearly clashes with itself is a favorit of many. And while offers were made to purchase any one of these, I could not bring myself to part with this set.

Nature's Fluidity, 2020

I love the stark contrast in this piece between the black and white, very clean line person and everything around them just an array of colors with a very fluid motion. This acrylic on canvas board was a product of the pandimic and too much time on my hands, a difficult time for me with many personal losses. This painting stirs many emotions for me.

Valkyrie Falling, 2021

This piece shares a similar to the last, an acrylic on canvas board, painted during the pandimic. this peice features a winged person falling amid a flaming sky, likely exhausted from an intensive battle. And another fine example of the toonish style I can't seem to get away from and the feeling of defeat and stress of the anti-LGBTQ+ agenda that was being perpetrated.
